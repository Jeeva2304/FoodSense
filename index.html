Momos

Momos are small, round dumplings that are typically filled with minced meat, such as chicken, pork, or lamb, or vegetables, such as spinach, cabbage, or potato. The dough is made with flour, water, and salt, and is rolled out into small circles, filled with the desired filling, and then sealed by twisting the edges of the dough. They are then steamed or fried and served with a variety of sauces and condiments. The fillings of momos are what make them so special. The meat filling is often mixed with spices, garlic, and onions, while the vegetarian filling may have a mix of veggies and paneer, the Indian cottage cheese. The sauces and condiments that accompany momos also add to their distinct flavour. Achar, a spicy tomato-based dipping sauce, and chutney, made from mint or coriander, are two popular accompaniments for momos.

They can be sweet, savory, thick, thin, small, or large, but they are all delicious! While there are many styles of pancakes, we like to keep it classic with traditional American-style pancakes at Kate's Kitchen. Pancakes have really stood the test of time with their extensive history all over the world. They hold a special place in our hearts, reminding us of comfort, family meals, and Saturday mornings.

Chole Puri

Chole Bhature also called Chana Bhatura is a dish originated initially within the northern a part of the Indian subcontinent. Now this dish is famous always in Northern India while having massive following in Punjab, India, with its very own variant. It is a combination of chana masala (highly spiced white chickpeas) and bhatura, a fried bread crafted from maida that is basically white flour. Chhole Bhature is frequently eaten as a breakfast dish, from time to time observed with lassi. However, it can additionally be avenue meals or a complete meal and can be accompanied by means of, for example, onions, carrot pickle, green chutney and achaar.
